## Part 1: Answer the following questions

1.	Differentiate Moral and Ethics.

Moral is the belief of an individual regarding the rights and wrongs. Ethics is the collection of acknowledgements on how a profession 
should be done.

2.	Is it true that as a Professional, one must maintain up to date skills and knowledge? Explain. 

Yes, because in the current era of IT technology, things are changing faster, skill and knowledge that works on the past might not work as 
well now. It is also important to keep up to date with the newest protocol especially regarding about cyber security for the safety of your 
user.

4.	Describe eight attributes of the software engineering code of ethics and professional practice recommended by ACM/IEEE-CS. 

Act consistently with public interest, act in best interest of client and employer, ensure products meet highest standards, maintain 
integrity in professional judgment, promote effective project management, advance the profession, be fair to and supportive of colleagues, 
participate in lifelong learning.

5.	Discuss the risks that companies may face with employee’s use (whether for their personal or business purposes) of social media.

Misuse of company resources to conflicts of interest and disparagement of others and causing bad publicity due to bad monitoring of employee 
in using company’s social media.

6.	Discuss the ethical issues faced by system developers.

Causing loss of information and resources that might result severely harmful for users, the public, or employers due to coding errors, or 
security issues.

7.	Personalization becomes important in a system to make them feel the developer cares about their needs. What are the ethical challenges
that are faced by the system developers when they want to improve software personalization?

Violation of copyrights, patents, trade secrets and the terms of license agreements. User might to use copyrighted asset in customizing 
their system to suit their needs.
 
8.	Software testing is part of standard development practices. Discuss how a software developer could plan for ethical testing.

Include all type of users in testing stages to provide safe and useful systems. Allocate sufficient time and budget for testing. Do not rush 
the testing phase to chase deadline.


## Part 2: Case Study

Lisa Jones owns her own consulting business and has several people working for her. Lisa is currently designing a database management system 
for the personnel office of ToyTimeInc., a mid-sized company that makes toys. Lisa has involved ToyTimeInc management in the design process 
from the start of the project. It is now time to decide about the kind and degree of security to build into the system. 

Lisa has described several options to the client. The client has decided to opt for the least secure system because the system is going to 
cost more than was initially planned, and the least secure option is the cheapest security option. Lisa knows that the database includes 
sensitive information, such as performance evaluations, medical records, and salaries. With weak security, she fears that enterprising 
ToyTimeInc employees will be able to easily access this sensitive data. Furthermore, she fears that the system will be an easy target for 
external hackers. Lisa feels strongly that the system should be more secure than it would be if the least secure option is selected.

Lisa has tried to explain the risks to ToyTimeInc, but the CEO, the CIO, and the Director of Personnel are all convinced that the cheapest 
security is what they want. 

Based on code of ethics that exist in IEEE-CS/ACM Joint Task Force on Software Engineering and Professional Practices, identify at least two 
clauses that are related/violated. 

Provide justification to your choice of clauses. 

Firstly, Lisa shall act consistently with the public interest. She should only approve software if she has a well-founded belief that it is 
safe, meets specifications, passes appropriate tests, and does not diminish quality of life, diminish privacy, or harm the environment. In 
this case, since Lisa believe that the database is not secure enough to hold sensitive information from the user, she should not approve 
this option.

Secondly, Lisa shall ensure that their products and related modifications meet the highest professional standards possible. She should only 
develop software and documents that respect privacy of those affected by software. In this case, since Lisa feels strongly that the system 
should be more secure than what the current option chosen by her client, she should not continue to work with the client.
