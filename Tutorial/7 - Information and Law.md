1. What are the main components of the copyleft license and how do they differ from traditional copyright licenses?

Copyleft is a licensing strategy that uses copyright law to ensure a work remains free and open, rather than restricting its use. It legally 
guarantees the freedom to use, study, share, and modify the software.

2. What are the differences between permissive and copyleft open-source licenses?

Permissive licenses maximize adoption by companies who want to use the code in commercial products without releasing their own proprietary
source code. Copyleft open-source licenses ensure the software remains free and open forever, preventing companies from "locking up"
community contributions.

3. What are the key components of Malaysia’s ICT legal framework and how do these components address issues related to data protection and cybersecurity?

The key components of Malaysia’s ICT legal framework include The Cyber Security Act 2024 (Act 854), Personal Data Protection Act 2010 
(PDPA), Computer Crimes Act 1997 (CCA), and Communications and Multimedia Act 1998 (CMA). PDPA address data protection by protecting 
individuals from misuse of their data by businesses. It enforces principles like consent, security, and data integrity. CCA and CMA address 
cybersecurity by criminalizes unauthorized access, spreading computer viruses, and wrongful communication of passwords/access codes. It 
provides the legal basis to prosecute cybercriminals.

4. How does the Principle of Data Security under the PDPA ensure the protection of personal data, and what are the key measures organizations must implement to comply with this principle?

PDPA prevent physical or digital misplacement of data, using data for purposes other than those consented to, unauthorized changes to the 
data that could lead to inaccuracies, information falling into the wrong hands, leaking data to third parties, and tampering with or 
deleting data without authorization. Key measures organizations must implement include using firewalls, intrusion detection systems (IDS), 
and regularly updated antivirus software, protecting data "at rest" and "in transit" using modern encryption standards, and storing physical 
files in locked cabinets and ensuring high-risk areas have entry and exit controls.
