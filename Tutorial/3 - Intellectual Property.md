## Part 1: Structured Questions: Answer the following questions

1.	Distinguish between patent, copyright and trademark.

A patent protects inventions, a copyright protects original creative works, and a trademark protects brand names, logos, and other marks 
that identify goods or services.

2.	What is the difference between the symbol TM and ®?

TM is used for unregistered marks to claim rights. ® can only be used after a trademark has been officially registered with a government 
intellectual property office.

3.	Identify the type of trademark represented by the images in (a) and (b). What is the difference between these two types of trademarks?
 	 
(a) is a certification mark, which is used to indicates that the product has been tested and meets specific standards. (b) is a collective 
mark, which is used to indicate membership in a specific organization, association, or group.

4.	Describe intellectual property rights.

Intellectual property rights are legal rights bestowed to the creators, mostly in the form of patents, trademarks, and copyright. It is used 
to protect the moral and economic rights of the creators, in addition to the creativity and dissemination of their work

5.	What happens when the copyright of a work expires?

The work enters the public domain, which means anyone can use, reproduce, or distribute the work without needing permission from the 
original copyright holder.

6.	How to register a trademark?

Conduct a search for similar existing marks, then file an application with the Malaysian Intellectual Property Corporation, MyIPO with 
details about the trademark and the goods or services it will cover. After examination and a period of publication for opposition, your 
trademark will be registered if successful, leading to a registration certificate.
 
## Part 2: Case Study on Intellectual Property Lawsuits

Read the information from the article link: https://corporate.astro.com.my/mediaroom-releases/two-commercial-enterprises-pay-damages-for-illegally-streaming-astro-content-one-individual-fined-for-selling-tv-boxes-with-unauthorised-astro-content

1.	Describe the cases highlighted in the article. 

Two commercial enterprises, an F&B outlet and a hotel were sued for illegally streaming its content. An individual was fined RM12,000 for 
selling TV Boxes with unauthorized Astro content.

2.	What were the outcomes of these cases?

The F&B outlet in KL and the boutique hotel in Cameron Highlands agreed to pay Astro a total of RM55,000 in damages, published a public 
apology in the media and provided an undertaking to cease and desist from infringing Astro’s copyright in any manner whatsoever. Both 
outlets have also agreed to an Astro commercial subscription worth RM53,000 in total. The individual pleaded guilty and was fined RM 12,000 
or on default imprisonment to 6 months.

3.	Identify two lessons learned from these cases.

We should not stream copyrighted work or unauthorized content illegally as it is a copyright infringement and could get you in jail or 
fined. Secondly, we should not sell other people works without their authorization.
