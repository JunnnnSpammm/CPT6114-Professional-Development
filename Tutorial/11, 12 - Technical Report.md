## Answer the following questions:

1. Define the term “technical communication”. Why is it important to an organization?

Technical communication involves the delivery of clear, consistent and factual information for safe and efficient use and effective 
comprehension by users. It is important to an organization because it makes information more usable and accessible to those who need that 
information. It can advance the goals of the companies and organization that employ them

2. Explain the advantages and disadvantages of written communication.

Advantages: Words can be deliberately selected. Disadvantages: It is permanent, so once it is in print somewhere, it’s very difficult to 
rectify any mistake

3. What are the differences between Spoken and Written communication? Present your answer in the form of a table.

|     Spoken                     |     Written                     |
|--------------------------------|---------------------------------|
|     Sound and tones            |     Words and sentences         |
|     Informal                   |     Formal                      |
|     Interactive                |     Non-interactive             |
|     Less accurate              |     More accurate               |
|     Spoken expresses action    |     Written expresses things    |

4. Differentiate between the following types of manuals. Who is the intended reader of the following manuals?

* User Manuals – To document ways to use the product or app by users – Product or app users
* Developer Manuals - Developers
* System Administration Manuals - Administrators
* Release Notes - Users

## Class Activity

Your group’s task: **Write a concise and clear minute meeting based on the information provided in this scenario. Ensure that all key 
points are covered and that action items are clearly specified.** There are many templates available for writing meeting minutes. Feel free 
to choose the format that best suits your style or needs. 

A certain amount of time will be allocated for your group to do the minute meeting. Later, your group is to present the minute meeting. When
you present your minute meeting, **mention the link for which template or example you followed**.

Scenario:

> You are part of the software development team at Innov8 Tech, a company specializing in tech- solutions. Your team recently had a brief 15-minute meeting on 10th September 2024 to discuss the upcoming launch of a major new feature for your flagship product, Innov8 Pro 2024.

Scenario Details:
* Feature Overview: The new feature, "Smart MirrorOp" aims to enhance real-time data synchronization across devices, providing users with a seamless experience when using Innov8 Pro 2024 on multiple platforms. This feature is a key selling point for the upcoming product update.
* Launch Date: The team has set the launch date for 10th December 2024. This date was chosen to align with a major tech conference where Innov8 Tech will showcase the new feature.
* Marketing Strategy: The marketing team plans to roll out a multi-channel promotional campaign. This includes email newsletters, social media posts, and a dedicated blog series. The campaign is scheduled to start on 15th October 2024 to build anticipation before the launch.
* Bug Fixes: During the meeting, it was noted that several critical bugs have been identified in the beta version of Smart MirrorOp. The development team has committed to fixing these bugs by 1st October 2024, to ensure the feature is stable before the launch.
* Team Roles and Responsibilities:
    1. Rajesh’s team: Tasked with coordinating efforts between the development and marketing teams. Rajesh will ensure that the marketing team has all necessary information and assets by 30th September 2024.
    2. Sarah’s team: Responsible for overseeing quality assurance, including the resolution of critical bugs. Sarah will provide a progress report on bug fixes by 25th September 2024.
    3. Leonard’s team: Assigned to prepare the launch presentation and demonstration for the feature. Leonard will draft the presentation and circulate it for feedback by 5th October 2024.
* Additional Notes: The team discussed potential risks associated with the feature launch, including possible delays in bug fixes and the need for additional testing. A follow-up meeting is scheduled on 7th October 2024 to review the final preparations and address any outstanding issues.
