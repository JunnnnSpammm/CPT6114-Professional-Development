## Answer the following questions:

1.	Differentiate between internet piracy and end-user piracy.

Internet piracy is when software is illegally downloaded from the Internet. End-user piracy is when someone makes unauthorized copies of 
software.


2.	Explain the term “ghost piracy”.

Ghost piracy is the act of remotely accessing unlicensed software illegally.

3.	Is Open-source software the same as Free Software? Explain your answer.

No, open-source software means that program source code made available for use or modification as users or other developers see fit. Free 
software means the program is using a license that allow users to run, copy, study, and change the software.

4.	What are trade secrets?

Trade secrets are confidential and valuable business information. It can provide a competitive advantage by being unknown to the public and
competitors. Thus, they are kept confidential to preserve their value.

5.	How does a trade secret differ from other types of intellectual property rights such as patent or copyright?

A trade secret protects confidential information for an indefinite period, as long as it remains secret and provides a competitive edge, 
whereas other types of intellectual property rights such as patent or copyright protect inventions for a limited time after public disclosure

6.	What measures can companies take to protect their trade secrets?

Companies can sign a non-disclosure agreement (NDAs) and give restricted access to their employees to maintain secrecy.

## Case Study 1: Working in an existing software company and protecting Intellectual Property

You have just joined a software application company that has been in operation for more than 3 years.  Purple Rain is a private limited 
company and for the financial year ending 2023, it achieved financial revenue more than USD 1.5 million. Purple Rain is a market leader in 
Oracle and Java based application development solution and services to businesses in Malaysia. The solutions provided cover a broad range of 
industries, ranging from the telecommunications, education and manufacturing sectors.  The company is looking into expanding their 
operations and you have been employed to provide client support and to continuously improve the “MYSTICZ” Solution System. The software 
application provides end-to-end solutions for manufacturing, logistics and telecommunications companies.

Meanwhile, you are also working with some of your ex-classmates from MMU on a mobile game that is similar to Subway Surfers.  Your friends 
and you intend to release the game on the Google Play Marketplace within the next 6 months.

### Discuss:

1.	What are the Intellectual Property considerations that Purple Rain should take into consideration (assuming that the company has taken no previous effort in protecting its Intellectual Property)  

Purple Rain must ensure that the source code for MYSTICZ is protected by copyright. Then, Purple Rain can implement a strict Non-Disclosure 
Agreements (NDAs) with all employees, contractors, and partners, to prevent business logic, proprietary algorithms, customer lists, and 
financial data from leaking to competitor.

2.	Should you or shouldn’t you attempt to protect your own Intellectual Property and if so, how would you go about it?

Yes, you should protect your own Intellectual Property. Firstly, you must keep a clear, timestamped record of all source code versions, and 
game assets. Then, you can register the game title and its logo as a trademark with MyIPO before launching to Google Play Marketplace. 
Lastly, you must draft a Partnership Agreement with your classmate to define the percentage of ownership between each partner, list down 
who own the IP rights to the game, and how will the revenue and costs be shared between each partner.
